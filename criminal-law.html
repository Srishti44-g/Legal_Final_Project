Criminal Law

Explore various aspects of Criminal Law including IPC, CrPC, NDPS, POCSO, and Cybercrime.

üìú Criminal Law of India

Criminal law is the body of law that deals with offenses against society and prescribes punishments for crimes. It aims to maintain law and order by punishing wrongdoers and ensuring justice for victims.

Major Governing Laws

  • Indian Penal Code (IPC), 1860

    The primary criminal code that defines crimes and prescribes punishments

  • Code of Criminal Procedure (CrPC), 1973

    Establishes the procedural aspects of investigation, trial, and punishment

  • Indian Evidence Act, 1872

    Sets rules for admitting and examining evidence in courts

Additional Special Laws

  • NDPS Act (Narcotic Drugs & Psychotropic Substances Act)

    Deals with drug-related offenses and their prevention

  • POCSO Act (Protection of Children from Sexual Offences)

    Specifically addresses sexual offenses against children

  • IT Act, 2000 (Cybercrime Laws)

    Covers electronic crimes and digital evidence

The IPC is the primary law that defines crimes and prescribes punishments. It is divided into 23 Chapters and 511 Sections, covering various offenses.

üîπ Classification of Offenses Under IPC


(A) Offenses Against the Human Body


  • Murder (Section 302) ‚Äì Killing a person with intent
  • Culpable Homicide (Section 299 & 304) ‚Äì Killing without intention
  • Attempt to Murder (Section 307) ‚Äì Unsuccessful attempt to kill
  • Causing Death by Negligence (Section 304A) ‚Äì Death due to reckless actions
  • Assault (Section 351-358) ‚Äì Causing fear of harm or injury
  • Hurt & Grievous Hurt (Sections 319-338) ‚Äì Physical injuries, severe injuries

(B) Offenses Against Women & Children


  • Rape (Section 375 & 376) ‚ÄìSexual assault against a woman
  • Sexual Harassment (Section 354A) ‚Äì Unwelcome advances, molestation
  • Dowry Death (Section 304B) ‚Äì Death due to dowry harassment
  • Cruelty (Section 498A) ‚Äì Death due to reckless actions
  • Assault (Section 351-358) ‚Äì Domestic violence against women
  • Child Abuse (POCSO Act, 2012) ‚ÄìProtection of children from sexual offenses

(C) Offenses Against Property


  • Theft (Section 378-382) ‚Äì Stealing movable property
  • Extortion (Section 383-389) ‚ÄìForcing someone to give property using fear
  • Robbery (Section 390-392) ‚Äì Theft with violence or threat
  • Dacoity (Section 395-398) ‚Äì Death due to reckless actions
  • Assault (Section 351-358) ‚Äì Robbery committed by five or more persons
  • Criminal Trespass (Section 441-447) ‚ÄìUnlawful entry into property

(D) Offenses Against Public Order


  • Unlawful Assembly & Rioting (Sections 141-160) ‚Äì Disturbing peace
  • Public Nuisance (Section 268-290) ‚Äì Actions affecting public life
  • Promoting Hatred (Section 153A, 295A) ‚Äì Spreading communal hatred

(E) White-Collar Crimes & Cyber Crimes


  • Cheating (Section 415-420) ‚Äì Fraudulent deception for gain
  • Forgery (Section 463-471) ‚Äì Creating fake documents
  • Cybercrime (IT Act, 2000) ‚Äì Hacking, phishing, identity theft
  • Money Laundering (PMLA Act, 2002) ‚ÄìConcealing illegal money

(F) Offenses Against the State


  • Sedition (Section 124A) ‚ÄìAnti-national activities
  • Treason (Section 121) ‚Äì Waging war against the country
  • Terrorism (UAPA Act, 1967) ‚ÄìActs threatening national security

5Ô∏è‚É£ Punishments Under Criminal Law


Punishments are given based on the severity of the crime.

  • Capital Punishment (Death Sentence) ‚ÄìRare cases (e.g., terrorism, brutal murder)
  • Life Imprisonment ‚Äì Rape, murder, treason
  • Imprisonment ‚ÄìRigorous (with hard labor) or simple (without hard labor)
  • A Fine ‚Äì Monetary penalty
  • Probation & Community Service ‚ÄìFor first-time offenders
